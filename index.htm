Game Engine Comparison (Free & Versatile First)

Guided breakdown of major game engines with focus on **free accessibility**, features, typical use-cases, and pros/cons.

Godot (Free & Open Source)

Cost: 100% Free (MIT License), no royalties

Best For

Indie developers, 2D games, lightweight 3D, learners, open-source enthusiasts.

Key Features
  • Dedicated 2D engine + capable 3D support
  • Built-in scripting (GDScript), also supports C# & C++
  • Portable export to mobile & web
  • No subscription or revenue share
Who Should Use It?

Beginners, hobbyists, educators, small teams who want freedom from licensing fees and full engine access.

Drawbacks

Smaller ecosystem than Unity/Unreal, 3D tools less advanced for AAA needs.

Roblox (Free Platform & Engine)

Cost: Free to use; earnings via platform revenue share

Best For

Social games, multiplayer experiences, monetizing within Roblox ecosystem.

Key Features
  • Built-in community & discovery
  • Lua scripting with Roblox Studio
  • Instant publishing & cross-platform access
  • Earnings through DevEx program
Who Should Use It?

Creators focused on community play, social interaction, and platform-centric monetization.

Drawbacks

Games only live within Roblox ecosystem; creative control is limited to platform rules.

Rapid multiplayer prototyping, creative social games, connected experiences.

Key Features

  • Designed for shared worlds and social interaction
  • Fast game publishing + discovery network

Unity (Free Tier Available)

Cost: Free for personal use under revenue threshold; Paid plans for professionals

Best For

2D & 3D games, mobile, VR/AR, indie to mid-sized commercial titles.

Key Features
  • C# scripting with strong IDE support
  • Huge asset marketplace & ecosystem
  • Wide platform export (PC, mobile, consoles, web)
  • Flexible for many game genres
Who Should Use It?

Indie studios, solo developers, cross-platform creators seeking robust tooling.

Drawbacks

Free tier has revenue limits; performance overhead in complex projects.

Unreal Engine (Free to Start with Royalties)

Cost: Free to use; royalty applies after revenue threshold

Best For

High-end 3D, AAA quality visuals, cinematic experiences.

Key Features
  • Top-tier graphics + visual fidelity
  • Blueprints (visual scripting) + C++ source access
  • Robust tools for film, simulation, architectural viz
Who Should Use It?

Teams targeting high graphical fidelity or big budget titles.

Drawbacks

Steeper learning curve; heavier system requirements.
